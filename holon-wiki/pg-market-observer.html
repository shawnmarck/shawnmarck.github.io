Market Observer — Perceiving Market Direction Through Noise EXPLAIN

TL;DR: A MarketObserver takes a candle-encoded thought vector, strips learned background noise via an online PCA subspace, and classifies the residual anomaly as Up or Down using a Reckoner. It never labels itself — the market is the judge. Each observer wears a MarketLens (vocabulary selection), maintains its own WindowSampler time scale, and gates its knowledge into long-term memory via an engram gate. One per asset-pair; N per trading system.

Anatomy#

Component Type Role
lens MarketLens Which vocabulary modules this observer attends to
reckoner Reckoner Discrete Up/Down classifier — the core prediction engine
noise_subspace OnlineSubspace 8-component online PCA. Learns the background distribution; strips it to expose anomaly
good_state_subspace OnlineSubspace 4-component subspace that learns what good discriminant states look like (for engram gating)
window_sampler WindowSampler Own time scale for window selection
incremental IncrementalBundle Optimization cache — maintains running sums across candles to avoid re-summing

Tracking fields: resolved (predictions settled), recalib_wins, recalib_total, last_recalib_count, last_prediction.

"Its own teacher. The market is the judge." — T0-013

Prediction (observe)#

There is no method literally named predict. Prediction lives in observe():

  1. Clone raw thought — preserved for downstream consumers who need both raw and cleaned vectors.
  2. Update noise subspace — the noise model absorbs the current thought as part of the background distribution (CCIPCA incremental PCA).
  3. Strip noise — anomalous_component() projects the thought onto the noise subspace and returns the residual. "The residual IS the signal." (Proposal 024).
  4. Classify the anomaly — Reckoner.predict() runs on the noise-stripped vector, not the raw one.
  5. Compute edge — reckoner.accuracy_at(conviction) yields the predicted accuracy at the current conviction level (0.0 if the conviction→accuracy curve is not yet valid).
  6. Store last_prediction — maps the reckoner's label index (0→Up, 1→Down) to a Direction.
  7. Return ObserveResult — containing raw_thought, anomaly, prediction (scores + conviction), and edge.

Key design: prediction and learning are aligned — both operate on the anomaly, not the raw thought.

ObserveResult#

Field Meaning
raw_thought The input before noise stripping
anomaly After noise subspace removal — the residual signal
prediction Scores + conviction from the Reckoner
edge accuracy_at(conviction) or 0.0

Learning (resolve)#

Called by broker propagation when a trade settles:

  1. Map direction to label — Direction::Up → index 0, Direction::Down → index 1.
  2. Check correctness — compares last_prediction against actual market direction.
  3. Reckoner learns — reckoner.observe(thought, label, weight) feeds the labeled experience to the discrete classifier, updating its accumulated prototypes.
  4. Feed the conviction curve — a second predict+resolve cycle updates the internal conviction→accuracy curve with the actual correctness signal. This lets the observer self-calibrate: "When I say I'm confident, am I actually right?"
  5. Engram gate check — accumulates wins and totals, then evaluates whether the observer has proven itself through check_engram_gate(). The gate uses good_state_subspace to learn what good discriminant states look like.

Noise Stripping — The Subspace Pipeline#

The noise_subspace is an OnlineSubspace (8 principal components) implementing CCIPCA (Weng et al., 2003):

  • Online PCA — incrementally learns the principal components of incoming thought vectors without storing them.
  • Adaptive threshold — EMA of residuals + σ-multiplier, calibrated before each CCIPCA update to match test-time scoring distribution.
  • Anomalous component — x - reconstruct(x): whatever the thought contains that the background model does not explain. This residual is what the Reckoner classifies.

"Returns the residual norm (anomaly score). Higher = more anomalous." — subspace.rs

The result: normal market chatter is projected out. What remains is the signal — structural shifts that the background model hasn't absorbed yet.

The Reckoner — Classification Engine#

The reckoner is a discrete-mode Reckoner with two labels: "Up" and "Down".

  • Accumulation — maintains running weighted sums of thought vectors per label (not individual observations). Frequency-preserving: patterns seen many times contribute proportionally more.
  • Discriminant — prototype(label) - prototype(other) computed at each recalibration. The classic Fisher-like linear separation direction.
  • Prediction — cosine similarity between the anomaly and each discriminant. The winning label is the one with the highest raw cosine; conviction = |cosine|.
  • Conviction curve — fits accuracy = (1/N) + a·exp(b·conviction) from resolved outcomes. Monotonically increasing = real signal. Used to compute the edge metric.

"The discriminant IS the reckoner's learned knowledge, externalized." — primitives.wat

The Lens — What the Observer Sees#

Each observer wears a MarketLens that determines which vocabulary modules are encoded into its thought vector. 11 variants across three schools:

School Lenses
Dow DowTrend, DowVolume, DowCycle, DowGeneralist
Pring PringImpulse, PringConfirmation, PringRegime, PringGeneralist
Wyckoff WyckoffEffort, WyckoffPersistence, WyckoffPosition

Each *Generalist is the union of its school's specialist lenses (superset of modules). Vocabulary modules include: momentum, persistence, regime, timeframe, flow, standard, keltner, oscillators, divergence, price_action, fibonacci. All lenses share shared::time (encoded once before the match).

Self-Evaluation — Proof Through Outcomes#

The observer's conviction→accuracy curve is its proof of reliability:

  • Requires ≥500 resolved predictions (default min_resolved).
  • Bins resolved outcomes by conviction, fits exponential model via log-linear regression.
  • A valid curve with positive b means: "higher conviction correlates with higher accuracy — the reckoner knows what it knows."
  • The edge metric (accuracy at current conviction, capped at 99%) drives downstream trading decisions.

Engram Gate — Earning Long-Term Memory#

The good_state_subspace (4 components) learns what good discriminant states look like. The engram gate evaluates:

  • Recalibration win rate — recalib_wins / recalib_total since last gate check.
  • Discriminant state quality — is the current internal state "good" by the subspace's model?

Observers that prove themselves get their knowledge gated into long-term memory (engrams). Those that don't are recycled.

Construction#

MarketObserver::new(lens, dims, recalib_interval, window_sampler)
  • Reckoner: discrete, two labels ("Up", "Down"), named "direction-{lens}".
  • noise_subspace: 8 principal components.
  • good_state_subspace: 4 principal components.
  • resolved starts at 0. last_prediction defaults to Down (arbitrary).
