# Seeds and Emergence

> **TL;DR:** Run named expert journals AND the full-vocabulary expert simultaneously. Named experts are seeds — human knowledge encoded as thought programs. Emergent experts are discovered through conviction-curve overlap. The geometry reveals the real structure.

## The Concept

Conventional wisdom says: "the Ichimoku expert," "the RSI momentum expert," "the Wyckoff volume expert." Each is a [[Market Observers|market observer]] with a specific [[Lenses|lens]] — a named vocabulary subset. These are the **seeds**.

But the real experts don't have names. They **emerge** from observation.

## How Emergence Works

When the Ichimoku observer and the RSI observer produce similar discriminants — when their conviction spikes on the same candles — that's not two experts agreeing. That's **one unnamed expert discovered through the overlap** of two named ones.

The superposition of named experts produces emergent unnamed experts. You don't name the groups. They name themselves through their [[Conviction-Accuracy Curve|conviction-accuracy curves]].

## Named vs Full Expert

The implementation runs both:
- **Named experts** — each lens variant, vocabulary subset
- **Full expert** — all vocabulary modules combined (the `:generalist` lens)

The named experts are hypotheses. The full expert is the null hypothesis:
- If a named expert's curve is steeper → that vocabulary subset contains **concentrated signal** — the named thought program is better than thinking everything at once
- If the full expert wins → the named subsets were arbitrary boundaries on a continuous thought space

Either way, you learn something. The curve judges.

## The N×M Grid as Exploration

The [[Brokers|broker]] grid (N market observers × M exit observers) is itself a seed-and-emergence machine:
- Each cell is a hypothesis: "this market thinking + this exit strategy produces Grace"
- The treasury funds based on edge
- High-edge brokers ARE the emerged experts
- Their {market-lens, exit-lens} identity tells you what combination works

## Philosophical Implication

The system doesn't need to know the "right" way to think about markets. It needs to try every way and measure which ones work. The expert is not designed — it is **discovered** through measurement.

This is the scientific method applied to cognitive architecture: hypothesize (named experts), measure (the curve), and let the results determine the theory (which experts are real).
