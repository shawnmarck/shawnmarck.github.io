Grace and Violence — the two outcomes of every paper trade. EXPLAIN

TL;DR

Every paper-trade resolves to exactly one of two outcomes: Grace or Violence. Grace means the trail was crossed — the prediction was confirmed and the trade captured value. Violence means the stop was hit — the prediction was wrong and the trade was cut. These are not profit/loss labels; they are accountability labels (Proposal 007). The broker accumulates them into dollar-P&L EMAs and an expected-value score that gates whether the broker is allowed to propose funded trades.

Grace and Violence — the two outcomes of every paper trade.

What They Are

OutcomeTriggerMeaningamount value
GraceTrail distance crossed (first time)Prediction confirmed — the trade reached its targetpaper.excursion() — the realized gain fraction

"Grace and Violence replace Win and Lose." — T-1-001-story.md (Proposal 007)

The distinction is structural, not emotional. Violence fires before Grace on any given tick — if both would resolve simultaneously, Violence wins and the paper is removed. (pg-broker)

The Three Resolution Paths

A paper-trade can resolve in exactly three ways:

Path 1: Stop fires before trail crosses → Violence

The price moves against the prediction and hits the stop distance. The paper emits a Violence resolution into market_signals for market-observer learning. No runner history is created.

Path 2: Trail crosses (first time) → Grace signal

The price moves in the predicted direction and crosses the trail distance. The paper emits a Grace signal into market_signals. This does not end the paper — it begins the runner phase. A RunnerHistory is created and per-candle accumulation begins.

Path 3: Runner trail fires → Grace resolution

The runner's trailing stop is eventually crossed (or the direction flips, triggering close_all_runners()). The paper emits a Grace resolution into runner_resolutions. This includes a position batch — deferred training data for the position-observer.

Dollar P&L Calculation

Each outcome is converted to dollar terms against a fixed reference capital of $10,000 and accounts for swap fees on both entry and exit.

Grace P&L


residue_usd = weight × 10_000          // weight IS excursion for Grace
entry_fee   = 10_000 × swap_fee
exit_fee    = (10_000 + residue_usd) × swap_fee
net         = residue_usd - entry_fee - exit_fee

Violence P&L


loss_usd    = weight × 10_000          // weight IS stop_distance for Violence
entry_fee   = 10_000 × swap_fee
exit_fee    = (10_000 - loss_usd) × swap_fee
net         = -(loss_usd + entry_fee + exit_fee)

EMA Tracking

Both outcomes update exponential moving averages with alpha = 0.038 (half-life ~50 papers):


avg_grace_net    = (1 - α) × avg_grace_net    + α × net   // Grace trades
avg_violence_net = (1 - α) × avg_violence_net + α × net   // Violence trades

Source: T0-018-broker-rust.md, §7.8

Expected Value and the Gate

The broker's expected_value is a weighted average:


grace_rate = grace_count / trade_count
expected_value = grace_rate × avg_grace_net + (1 - grace_rate) × avg_violence_net
  1. Cold start — if either outcome count is below 50, the gate is always open (not enough data to judge)
  2. Warm — after ≥50 of both outcomes, the gate opens only when expected_value > 0.0

A broker that costs money on average is suppressed — it cannot propose funded trades.

"Pure accounting + gate + log." — T0-018-broker-rust.md, §11 (Proposal 035)

Direction Flips and Forced Grace

When the market-observer's active direction flips (e.g., was predicting Up, now predicts Down), close_all_runners() force-resolves all active runners with Outcome::Grace. Non-runner papers that haven't resolved survive the flip — they'll hit their stop naturally.
