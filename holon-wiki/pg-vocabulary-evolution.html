Vocabulary Evolution — how the system's words emerged and changed

TL;DR: The project's vocabulary grew from ~7 algebraic primitives (bind, bundle, prototype, difference, blend, amplify, negate) forged in a failed Sudoku solver, through domain-specific terms (engram, decay model, residual profile), to a 200+ atom named-thought vocabulary judged by the Reckoner. Each naming decision was explicit, often painful, and always measured. Words that produced Grace survived; words that produced Violence were reaped. The LLM's bias was recognized as the vocabulary supply — the human's job was naming; the machine's job was judging.

Phase 0: The Primitives (Jan 2026)

The first vocabulary was not a vocabulary at all — it was an algebra. Seven operations emerged from Batch 004's failed Sudoku attempt:

PrimitiveRole
bindAssociate a label with a vector (structural composition)
bundleSuperpose vectors (superposition / wave function)
prototypeCentroid of a set (learned norm)
differenceResidual from prototype (anomaly signal)
blendWeighted interpolation
amplifyScale a vector
negateInvert a vector
"The primitives forged in the attempt were the real output, not the Sudoku solver." — T-1-001-story, Series 002-002

These seven operations constituted the "primitive algebra" — proven across tasks, recipes, graph topology, and text, but structurally incapable of exact combinatorial search. The NP Wall was the first lesson: vocabulary has a domain, and honest naming means naming the boundary.

Source: T-1-001-story, Series 002-001 and 002-002.

Phase 1: Production Vocabulary — L4/L7 Security (Feb 6–28)

From Python ceiling to Rust primitives

The Rust port (Feb 6) didn't add vocabulary — it validated it. 239 Python tests served as "Rosetta Stone" behavioral spec. The vocabulary remained the same; the words just ran 10–15× faster.

Domain words emerge

Moving from algebra to production introduced domain-specific vocabulary:

The expression tree (Feb 27–28)

The biggest vocabulary shift in the security phase. Rules went from 13 hard-coded FieldDim variants to composable s-expressions over three orthogonal concept families:

  • Domain accessors (26 dimensions): header, path, method, tls-version…
  • Generic functions (10 operations): first, last, nth, count, keys, vals, set, lower…
  • Operators (10 predicates): =, prefix, suffix, contains, regex, gt, lt, subset, superset…
"No production router or firewall I know of can host a million rules while maintaining sub-microsecond evaluation." — T-1-001-story, Series 004-002

The expression tree compiler was dimension-agnostic — new words could be added without touching the evaluation engine. This was the first sign that vocabulary scaling was a first-class design concern.

Source: T-1-001-story, Series 003-001 through 005-003.

Phase 2: The Market Vocabulary (Mar 15–28)

From encoding to naming

The market phase introduced the most radical vocabulary expansion. The key insight: encoding raw data (pixels, candles) produced noise; encoding named interpretations produced signal.

"Charts don't predict; interpretations of charts predict. Visual engrams are a dead end." — T-1-001-story, Series 006-001

Vocabulary as model

Named thoughts became a measurable, scalable resource:

The LLM's bias as vocabulary supply

The book makes this explicit: the LLM's training data provides hundreds of indicator concepts. The inscribe agent names them. The Reckoner judges them. Natural selection operates on the LLM's bias.

"The LLM's bias IS the vocabulary expansion. LLM training data provides hundreds of indicator concepts. The inscribe agent names them. The Reckoner judges them. Natural selection on the LLM's bias." — T-1-001-story, Series 006-006

Fact interface (Mar 29)

The Fact interface formalized vocabulary as architecture. 13 vocab modules across 1,060 lines, each returning Vec. Modules don't touch vectors.

"Adding a new vocabulary module means writing a function that takes candles and returns Vec. No vector knowledge required." — T-1-001-story, Series 006-004

Phase 3: The Philosophical Vocabulary (Mar 31–Apr 7)

Grace and Violence

The most consequential naming decision. Labels were "lies" — a trade up 3% then crashed to -1% was "Win" because threshold was crossed first. 91% of candles labeled "Loss" not because the market went down, but because the threshold taxonomy was wrong.

Grace and Violence replaced Win and Lose as accountability labels. These are not value judgments — they are measurements of whether a thought produced prosperity or destruction.

"What was needed was a machine that can only discern: did this thought produce prosperity or violence? Not divination — measurement. Not truth handed down — truth measured from outcomes." — T-1-002-book

Booleans as premature measurement

The boolean was identified as a structural lie. It collapses the wave function at the vocabulary level, before the discriminant has a chance to measure. Scalars preserve the superposition until the Reckoner is ready.

"The boolean collapsed the wave function at the vocabulary. The scalar preserves it until the discriminant." — T-1-002-book

The Reckoner

The Journal was killed on Easter Sunday. The Reckoner was born — a unified primitive with discrete and continuous readout. The final vocabulary consolidation: five primitives total.

"The Journal is killed. The Reckoner is born. On Easter Sunday — the day of resurrection." — T-1-002-book

Phase 4: Specification as Vocabulary (Apr 4–7)

The Guide and the Disposable Machine

The Guide (2,138 lines) became the definitive vocabulary — every struct, every interface, every dependency, every construction order. The wat specification language (37 files, 4,804 lines) inscribed the guide as executable s-expressions.

"The guide is the DNA. The spells are the ribosomes. The wat is the protein. The Rust is the organism." — T-1-001-story, Series 006-006

The key property: f(guide) = guide. The ignorant ward applied to the guide produces the guide. No changes needed. The vocabulary achieved a fixed point.

Eight automated skills police vocabulary honesty:

213 phantom runes (forms that don't exist in the language) were dissolved to zero. The LLM replaced phantoms with new phantoms (6 new ones) — "fluently, and wrong."

Source: T-1-001-story, Series 006-004 through 006-006; T-1-002-book, Act IV.

Cross-Cutting Pattern: Naming Is Architecture

The recurring discovery across all phases: the vocabulary is not a layer on top of the system — it IS the system.

  • Phase 0: Seven primitives defined what the algebra could express
  • Phase 1: Domain accessors defined what the rule engine could match
  • Phase 2: Named thoughts defined what the discriminant could predict
  • Phase 3: Grace/Violence defined what accountability could measure
  • Phase 4: The Guide defined what the specification could generate

Each phase added words, but the selection mechanism was the same: measure, don't believe. Words that produced signal survived. Words that produced noise were reaped. The 405 lines of dead code removed in the Datamancer phase — removed not through optimization, but through vocabulary correction.

"Honest architecture produces honest results." — T-1-001-story, Series 006-004
