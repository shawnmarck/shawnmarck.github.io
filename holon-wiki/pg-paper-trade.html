Paper Trade — hypothetical accountability without execution EXPLAIN

TL;DR

A paper trade is a simulated position that never executes on a real market. It tracks a single predicted direction (Up or Down) against live prices to measure whether the market observer's prediction was correct — producing binary outcomes (Grace or Violence) that drive all learning in the system. Paper trades are owned by Broker instances, created every candle, and resolved when either a trailing stop or a fixed stop loss fires. All learning signals — market observer corrections, position observer batch training, scalar accumulator updates, P&L accounting — originate from paper trade resolutions. No real capital is ever at risk; the entire mechanism is "hypothetical accountability."

Lifecycle

flowchart TD
    created["Created\n(register_paper)"] -->|"price moves against prediction\nhits fixed stop"| violence["Violence\n(resolved, never signaled)"]
    created -->|"price moves in predicted direction\nfar enough to cross trail threshold"| runner["signaled = true\nnow a Runner"]
    runner -->|"price reverses\ncrosses trailing stop"| grace["resolved\n(Grace — was signaled)"]
    runner -->|"price continues favorably"| ratchet["extreme/trail ratchet\nkeeps running"]
    created -->|"price never moves enough\nin either direction"| open["stays open indefinitely"]

Each paper is created at the candle's entry price. On every subsequent candle, tick(current_price) is called. The paper is removed once resolved.

Source: T0-015 § Lifecycle Summary; T0-018 § 7.7

Resolution Classifications

OutcomeConditionMeaning
Gracesignaled == true — trail crossed at some pointMarket confirmed the prediction.
Violenceresolved && !signaled — stop fired before trail ever crossedPrediction was wrong.
Runnersignaled && !resolved — trail crossed, still runningNot yet resolved.

These are mutually exclusive in any given state. An unsignaled, unresolved paper matches none of the three.

Source: T0-015 § Resolution Classifications

Stop priority

Violence is checked before Grace on each tick. If both triggers would fire simultaneously, Violence wins — a price that hits the stop and the trail-cross threshold on the same tick is classified as Violence, not Grace.

Source: T0-015 § tick — "Key ordering"

Triggers

Trail (Grace)

A trailing stop that follows the best price in the predicted direction:

  • Up predictions: trail_level = extreme - extreme × trail_distance. Only ratchets upward.
  • Down predictions: trail_level = extreme + extreme × trail_distance. Only ratchets downward.

The extreme tracks the best price seen so far (monotonically favorable). The trail level follows with a gap of trail_distance percent.

Stop (Violence)

A fixed stop loss set at creation, never moves:

  • Up predictions: entry - entry × stop_distance (below entry).
  • Down predictions: entry + entry × stop_distance (above entry).

Excursion

excursion() measures how far price moved in the predicted direction, as a fraction of entry:

  • Up: (extreme - entry) / entry
  • Down: (entry - extreme) / entry

Always ≥ 0. Zero at creation. Used as the "amount" in Grace resolutions — the realized gain fraction.

Source: T0-015 § excursion()

Broker Integration

Creation

The Broker creates a paper every candle via register_paper(), which assigns a monotonic paper_id and pushes the paper onto a VecDeque. Three separate thoughts are recorded: composed, market, and position (Proposal 026 — position observer learns from its own thought, not composed).

Source: T0-018 § 7.5

Ticking

tick_papers() returns two vectors:

  1. market_signals — Grace signals and Violence stops, for market observer learning.
  2. runner_resolutions — completed runners with position batch data, for position observer + broker learning.

When a paper first signals Grace (trail crosses), a RunnerHistory is created and the broker begins accumulating per-candle thoughts, distances, and prices. When the runner resolves, compute_position_batch() produces deferred batch training data using a suffix-extremum pass and sparse sampling (only when optimal trail changed by >10% relative).

Source: T0-018 § 7.7

Direction Flip

When the market observer's active direction flips, close_all_runners() force-resolves all active runners as Grace. Non-runner papers that haven't resolved survive the flip (they'll hit stop naturally).

Source: T0-018 § 7.6

From Resolution to Learning

Resolutions flow through propagate(), which performs three actions:

  1. Track record — updates cumulative grace/violence and counts.
  2. Scalar accumulator learning — accumulators observe the optimal (hindsight) distances, weighted by outcome, teaching what trail/stop would have been ideal.
  3. Dollar P&L + EMA — against a fixed $10,000 reference capital with swap fees on entry and exit. Grace P&L uses excursion as the gain fraction; Violence P&L uses stop distance as the loss fraction. EMA alpha = 0.038 (half-life ~50 papers). Produces expected_value = grace_rate × avg_grace_net + (1 - grace_rate) × avg_violence_net.
Source: T0-018 § 7.8

The Gate

Paper trade outcomes power the broker's gate via gate_open():

  • Cold start (< 50 of either outcome): gate always open — not enough data.
  • Warm (≥ 50 of both): gate open only when expected_value > 0.0.

A broker with negative expected value is suppressed — it cannot propose funded trades.

Source: T0-018 § 7.3

Distance Cascade

Trail and stop distances come from a three-tier fallback:

TierSourceCondition
1Reckoner answer (position observer)Some(dists) → return immediately
2ScalarAccumulator extractionIf accumulator has observations. Clamped to [0.1%, 10%].
3Default distancesConstructor-provided fallback.
Source: T0-018 § 7.4
