Paper Trade — hypothetical accountability without execution EXPLAIN

TL;DR#

A paper trade is a simulated position that never executes on a real market. It tracks a single predicted direction (Up or Down) against live prices to measure whether the market observer's prediction was correct — producing binary outcomes (Grace or Violence) that drive all learning in the system. Paper trades are owned by the Treasury, created every candle by broker submission, and resolved when a deadline fires. The broker discovers outcomes by reading paper state — it does not own or tick papers itself. All learning signals — market observer corrections, broker gate reckoner training — originate from paper trade resolutions. No real capital is ever at risk; the entire mechanism is "hypothetical accountability."

Architecture note (Proposal 056): Paper trades were previously owned by the broker (register_paper, tick_papers, close_all_runners). The broker was stripped to essentials and the treasury now owns all paper lifecycle management. The broker submits proposals and reads state via TreasuryHandle.

Lifecycle#

flowchart TD
    created["Created\n(treasury.submit_paper)"] -->|deadline fires| resolved["Resolved"]
    resolved -->|price moved in predicted direction| grace["Grace — paper lived"]
    resolved -->|price moved against prediction| violence["Violence — deadline hit"]
    broker["Broker discovers outcome\n(treasury.get_paper_state)"] -->|Grace| record_grace["record_outcome(Grace)"]
    broker -->|Violence| record_violence["record_outcome(Violence)"]

Removed: The old lifecycle had tick_papers(), trail_distance, stop_distance, RunnerHistory, and compute_position_batch(). All stripped in the backlog sweep. The treasury now handles paper lifecycle with simple deadline-based resolution.

Resolution Classifications#

Outcome Condition Meaning
Grace Paper survived to deadline with favorable outcome Market confirmed the prediction.
Violence Deadline hit, unfavorable outcome Prediction was wrong.

Broker Integration (Post-056)#

Creation#

The Broker submits a paper proposal every candle via treasury.submit_paper(from_asset, to_asset, price). The treasury decides whether to fund it. If approved, the broker receives a PositionReceipt.

Discovery#

The broker iterates its active_receipts, calling treasury.get_paper_state(paper_id) for each:

  • Active → keep receipt
  • Grace → broker.record_outcome(Grace), remove receipt
  • Violence → broker.record_outcome(Violence), remove receipt
  • None → remove receipt (unknown state)

Learning Flow#

  1. Broker records outcome — record_outcome(Grace) or record_outcome(Violence) updates counts and grace rate.
  2. Broker's gate reckoner learns — On gate 4 resolution, the gate reckoner observes the anomaly with the correct Hold/Exit label.
  3. Market observer learns — Through broker propagation on resolution.

What was removed: The old propagate() method that returned PropagationFacts. Scalar accumulator learning (trail_distance, stop_distance). Dollar P&L computation with EMA. cascade_distances() with three-tier fallback. close_all_runners() on direction flip. All gone. The broker calls record_outcome() and the gate reckoner learns from anomaly.

The Gate#

Paper trade outcomes power the broker's gate via gate_open():

  • Cold start (< 50 of either outcome): gate always open — not enough data.
  • Warm (≥ 50 of both): gate open only when expected_value > 0.5.

A broker with a 50/50 or worse grace rate is suppressed — it cannot propose funded trades.

Source: T0-018 § gate_open

Design Principles#

Principle Source Quote
No real execution T0-015 "It does not execute."
Treasury owns papers T0-018 "The broker submits papers and reads state."
Values up, not effects down T0-018 "All outputs flow upward as return values — no shared mutation."
Broker doesn't touch observers T0-018 "No shared observer references. No PropagationFacts."
Simple grace rate T0-018 "grace_count / trade_count. No EMA, no dollar P&L."
