Broker — the accountability primitive EXPLAIN

TL;DR: The broker binds one market observer to one regime observer in an N×M grid. It owns a state machine: outcome counts, a grace-rate gate, a noise subspace, and a gate reckoner (Hold/Exit). The broker submits papers to the treasury, discovers outcomes by reading paper state, composes a full thought (market + regime + portfolio + phase + time), and predicts Hold/Exit from the anomaly. 10 fields, 4 methods, 6 tests. No paper trades, no accumulators, no PropagationFacts, no EMA, no runner history — all stripped.

What it is#

"The accountability primitive. Binds one market observer + one regime observer. The broker owns the game — gate 4, anxiety atoms, exit/hold decisions. The treasury owns papers and capital." — broker.rs

The broker sits at the intersection of a market observer (which predicts direction) and a regime observer (which enriches with regime vocabulary and rhythms). It receives chains from the post, submits papers to the treasury, checks outcomes, composes the full thought, and predicts Hold/Exit via gate 4.

Crucially:

  • The broker submits but the treasury owns papers and capital.
  • The broker records outcomes via record_outcome() — simple counts, no PropagationFacts.
  • The broker gates itself with a grace-rate threshold (> 0.5).
  • The broker composes the full thought: market rhythms + regime rhythms + portfolio rhythms + phase + time.
  • The broker predicts Hold/Exit from the anomaly of the composed thought via the gate reckoner.

The N×M Grid#

Brokers live in a flat array indexed by slot_idx. With N market observers and M regime observers:

slot_idx = market_idx × M + regime_idx
market_idx = slot_idx / M       (integer division)
regime_idx = slot_idx % M

Each broker's slot_idx is its identity — it determines which market observer and which regime observer it binds. For example: slot_idx=5, M=3 → market_idx=1, regime_idx=2.

Methods (4 total)#

Broker::new(observer_names, slot_idx, regime_count, dims, recalib_interval)#

Constructor. Asserts regime_count > 0 (divide-by-zero guard). Five constructor args. Creates noise subspace (32 components) and gate reckoner (Discrete: Hold/Exit).

market_idx() / regime_idx()#

Grid derivation from slot_idx and regime_count. Pure integer math.

gate_open()#

Two-phase gate: cold start (< 50 of either outcome → always open), warm (≥50 of both → expected_value > 0.5). Threshold is strictly greater than 0.5.

record_outcome(outcome)#

Simple grace rate. No EMA, no dollar P&L.

Thought Composition (Proposal 056)#

The broker_program composes one full thought per candle:

market rhythms (the market observer's full AST)
+ regime rhythms (each one an indicator rhythm AST from regime observer)
+ portfolio rhythms (5 portfolio indicators as rhythm streams)
+ phase rhythm (bundled bigrams of trigrams with structural deltas)
+ time facts (hour, day-of-week — top-level, not rhythms)

All bundled into one ThoughtAST::Bundle. Encoded once. Noise subspace strips background. Gate reckoner predicts Hold/Exit from the anomaly.

Gate 4 — Now Wired#

  1. Compose full thought → encode → noise subspace → anomaly
  2. gate_reckoner.predict(anomaly) → Hold (label 0) or Exit (label 1)
  3. If Exit and gate is open → propose exit to treasury
  4. On resolution: gate_reckoner.observe(anomaly, label, weight) — the reckoner learns

Verified Properties#

  1. No shared mutation. The broker owns its 10 fields and mutates only itself.
  2. Gate has two distinct modes with a clear boundary at 50 resolved trades.
  3. expected_value is simple grace rate — grace_count / trade_count.
  4. Gate threshold is > 0.5, not > 0.0.
  5. 6 tests cover construction, grid math, cold-start gate, low-grace gate, high-grace gate, and outcome recording.
  6. Noise subspace learns normal composed thoughts — anomaly drives gate 4.
  7. Gate reckoner is Discrete Hold/Exit — learned from anomaly, not raw thought.
